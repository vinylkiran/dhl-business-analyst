# A/B Test Design Document — DHL At Risk Retention Campaign

**Author:** Vinyl Kiran Anipe (BA/DA)  
**Date:** June 2024  
**Audience:** Commercial Manager, Customer Success Lead, Finance  
**Status:** Ready for approval

---

## 1. Business Objective

DHL's At Risk customer segment — customers who were previously frequent buyers but have
not placed an order recently — represents recoverable revenue. The objective of this
campaign is to re-engage At Risk customers and measure, with statistical confidence,
whether a targeted retention offer meaningfully increases the probability of a repeat
order within 90 days.

The campaign will be run as a controlled experiment (A/B test) so that:
- We can attribute any increase in orders *specifically* to the campaign rather than
  to seasonal demand or other external factors.
- We can justify the campaign cost using evidence, not assumption.
- We establish a repeatable measurement framework for future campaigns.

---

## 2. Hypothesis

**Null hypothesis (H₀):** Sending the retention offer to At Risk customers has no
effect on their 90-day re-order rate compared to At Risk customers who receive no offer.

**Alternative hypothesis (H₁):** At Risk customers who receive the retention offer
will place at least one order within 90 days at a higher rate than those who do not.

We are testing whether the offer *works* — and we need the data to prove it either way.

---

## 3. Campaign Design

### Who Is Being Tested

The test targets only the **At Risk** RFM segment. These are customers who:
- Have placed **three or more orders** historically (R_Score ≥ 3)
- Have **not placed an order recently** (R_Score = 2 — approximately 60–400 days
  since their last order)

They are *not* lost customers — they have an established relationship with DHL.
They are customers whose engagement has dropped and who, with the right prompt,
are likely to return.

### What the Retention Offer Includes

*(To be finalised by Commercial Manager — the following is the proposed structure)*

The offer should address the most likely reasons for disengagement. Based on the
RFM profile analysis, OTIF performance is not the primary factor (performance is
consistent across segments). The offer should therefore address pricing or contract terms:

- Option A: **Priority SLA upgrade** — offer a temporary SLA improvement for the next
  three orders at no additional cost.
- Option B: **Volume discount** — offer a 5–8% discount on the next two orders above a
  minimum order value threshold.
- Option C: **Account review call** — a personal outreach from the account manager to
  understand why order frequency has dropped and offer to adjust contract terms.

**Recommendation:** Option C (account review call) for the top 20 At Risk customers
by historical revenue; Option B (volume discount) for the remainder.

### Test vs Control

| Group | Definition | Action |
|---|---|---|
| **Test** | ~50% of At Risk customers (randomly selected) | Receives the retention offer |
| **Control** | Remaining ~50% of At Risk customers | Business as usual — no offer |

Random assignment is 50/50 using a documented random seed to ensure reproducibility.
The two groups have been verified to be balanced on recency, frequency, monetary value,
and average order value.

---

## 4. Metrics

### Primary Metric (what we are trying to move)

**Conversion rate:** The percentage of customers in each group who place at least one
order within 90 days of campaign launch.

- This is a binary outcome: each customer either converted (1) or did not (0).
- A lift in conversion rate in the test group, when statistically significant, is
  evidence that the campaign caused the change.

### Guardrail Metric (what we must not break)

**Average order value (AOV):** The average revenue per order placed by converting
customers in each group.

- If the retention offer causes customers to place smaller, lower-margin orders just
  to take the discount, the campaign destroys value even if conversion rate improves.
- The campaign is considered a success *only if* conversion rate improves AND average
  order value does not significantly decrease.

### Secondary Metrics (tracked but not decision-drivers)

- 90-day revenue generated by each group.
- OTIF rate of new orders placed by re-engaged customers.
- Time-to-first-order after campaign launch (latency of response).

## 5. Sample Size and Test Duration

### Sample Size

The At Risk segment contains **[N] customers** at the time of analysis. This is split
50/50 into test and control groups, giving approximately **[N/2] customers per arm**.

Statistical analysis confirms that this sample size is sufficient to detect a
**minimum detectable effect of [MDE]%** at 80% power and α=0.05. If the campaign
achieves at least a [MDE]% lift in conversion rate, the test will have enough statistical
power to confirm or reject the hypothesis with confidence.

*(MDE and exact N populated after running ab_test.py — see `outputs/ab_test_readout.txt`)*

### Test Duration

**Recommended duration: 90 days from campaign launch date.**

Rationale: At Risk customers have average recency of 60–400 days. A 90-day window gives
sufficient time to observe a meaningful response from the segment without extending into
the next quarter's budgeting cycle. Measuring at 30 days would be too early; 180 days
introduces too much external variable noise.

Do not look at the results before 90 days. Peeking at interim results and stopping early
when the test looks good inflates the false positive rate.

---

## 6. Success Criteria

The campaign is a success if **all three** of the following conditions are met:

1. **Primary metric improves:** Test group conversion rate is higher than control group.
2. **Statistically significant:** The difference has a p-value < 0.05 on a two-sided
   z-test (equivalently, the 95% confidence interval on the lift does not include zero).
3. **Guardrail holds:** Average order value in the test group is not significantly lower
   than the control group (p > 0.05 on a Welch's t-test).

If all three conditions are met → **Launch the campaign at scale to all At Risk customers.**

If the primary metric improves but the guardrail fails → **Do not launch.** Redesign the
offer to remove the incentive to place smaller orders.

If the primary metric does not reach significance → **Do not launch.** Either the campaign
does not work, or the sample is too small. Extend duration or increase sample before deciding.

---

## 7. Risks and Mitigations

| Risk | Likelihood | Impact | Mitigation |
|---|---|---|---|
| Control group learns about the offer from test group contacts | Medium | High — contaminates control | Assign test/control by account manager team, not by customer. Instruct AMs not to share offer details with non-selected customers. |
| External factor (e.g. price increase, competitor campaign) affects both groups during test window | Low | Medium | Both groups are equally exposed — the test design controls for this as long as both groups are affected equally. |
| Test runs during an atypical month (e.g. Q4 peak) | Medium | Low | Peak months increase absolute conversion in both groups. The *difference* is still valid as long as seasonality is consistent. Note the timing in the results report. |
| Insufficient conversions to reach statistical significance | Medium | Medium | Calculated MDE and power analysis confirm the current sample is adequate for the simulated 15% uplift. If actual uplift is smaller, extend the test window by 30 days before drawing conclusions. |
| Finance cannot attribute campaign cost to individual account | Low | Medium | Agree on campaign cost code before launch and track offer acceptance vs order placement separately. |

## 8. Measurement and Reporting

- **Data capture:** Record the campaign group assignment (test/control) for each customer
  in the CRM or a shared tracking spreadsheet before launch.
- **Order tracking:** Pull order records from WMS at the 90-day mark. Match by Customer_ID.
- **Analysis owner:** BA/DA team (Vinyl Kiran Anipe) will run the post-campaign statistical
  analysis within one week of the 90-day mark.
- **Results presentation:** Full statistical readout delivered to Commercial Manager and
  Finance within two weeks of test completion.
